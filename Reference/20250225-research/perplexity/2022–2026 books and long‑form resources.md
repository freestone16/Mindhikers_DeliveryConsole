## 1. YouTube operations & growth

## 1) _Content Operations 2.0: How to Build and Scale Your Content Ops_ – Emilia Korczynska (2022)

- **Key takeaway:** Playbook from a SaaS company that went from 4 to 100 posts/month, detailing content epics, hierarchy, roles, and SOPs for scalable production.goodreads+1
    
- **Relevance:** While blog‑focused, the system (epics, editors, SOPs, Asana workflows, removal of single points of failure) adapts well to YouTube pipelines: idea → brief → script → edit → QA → publish.linkedin+1
    
- **eBook?:** Yes, available on Amazon Kindle as an ebook.[[linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/emiliakorczynska_content-operations-in-a-nutshell-activity-7013122373465878528-Dr7B)]​
    

## 2) _Leading Content Design_ – Rachel McConnell (A Book Apart, 2022)

- **Key takeaway:** Frameworks for building content teams, workflows, and standards inside digital product and media environments.[[abookapart](https://abookapart.com/products/leading-content-design.html)]​
    
- **Relevance:** Strong on operations and governance for content at scale; very useful for defining roles, review processes, and quality bars for a YouTube “studio” with multiple collaborators.[[abookapart](https://abookapart.com/products/leading-content-design.html)]​
    
- **eBook?:** Yes, A Book Apart sells it in ebook formats (EPUB/MOBI/PDF).[[abookapart](https://abookapart.com/products/leading-content-design.html)]​
    

## 3) Long‑form: _What Makes Content Operations Successful in the Age of AI?_ (Content Science report, 2025)

- **Key takeaway:** 95‑page research report tying content‑ops maturity levels to measurable content success, with a section on AI in content workflows.[[review.content-science](https://review.content-science.com/what-is-the-state-of-content-operations-in-2025/)]​
    
- **Relevance:** Gives you a maturity model to benchmark your YouTube/content operation and highlights where AI and process investments produce outsized gains.[[review.content-science](https://review.content-science.com/what-is-the-state-of-content-operations-in-2025/)]​
    
- **eBook?:** Free PDF report (downloadable; suitable to index in your own library).[[review.content-science](https://review.content-science.com/what-is-the-state-of-content-operations-in-2025/)]​
    

> Note: For direct, system‑level YouTube algorithm explanation post‑2023, most depth is in official videos and long articles rather than new books. Creator Insider with Todd (Director of Growth & Discovery at YouTube) is the canonical “algorithm science” resource.[[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=67TkpHKv9tE)]​

## 2. Content operations at scale

## 4) _Content Operations 2.0_ – Emilia Korczynska (2022)

- **Key takeaway:** Concrete walkthrough of scaling content from 1–10+ pieces/week: content audit, hierarchy, “content epics” workflow, editor KPIs, programmatic SEO.goodreads+1
    
- **Relevance:** Directly applicable to building an automated delivery system: it covers staffing model, throughput math (posts/editor), SOP design, and tooling patterns you can translate to video.goodreads+1
    
- **eBook?:** Yes (Kindle).[[goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/61381561-content-operations-2-0)]​
    

## 5) _AI for Creative Production_ – Ignacio De Pedro Crespo (Packt, 2023)

- **Key takeaway:** Handbook for using AI to create/process text, images, video, and audio with an emphasis on ethical, production‑grade workflows.[[packtpub](https://www.packtpub.com/en-us/product/ai-for-creative-production-9781806025800)]​
    
- **Relevance:** Bridges operations and AI: shows how to integrate AI across stages (ideation, drafting, editing, post‑production) in a way that is systematizable and team‑friendly.[[packtpub](https://www.packtpub.com/en-us/product/ai-for-creative-production-9781806025800)]​
    
- **eBook?:** Yes, sold explicitly as an ebook.[[packtpub](https://www.packtpub.com/en-us/product/ai-for-creative-production-9781806025800)]​
    

## 6) Article: “Content Operations, Decoded: Revisiting B2B Content Ops” – Relato (2026)

- **Key takeaway:** Clear definition of content ops as the network of people, processes, and technology; emphasizes templates, guides, and visibility into performance.[[relato](https://www.relato.com/blog/content-operations-decoded)]​
    
- **Relevance:** Short but useful for designing your operating model: roles, process documentation, and feedback loops between performance and production.[[relato](https://www.relato.com/blog/content-operations-decoded)]​
    
- **eBook?:** Web article; you can save as PDF for your library.

- **eBook?:** Routledge titles are typically released in ebook format; listing indicates ebook availability.[[routledge](https://www.routledge.com/AI-and-the-Creative-Economy-Transforming-Content-Creation-and-Influencer-Entrepreneurship/Boateng-Boateng-Anning-Dorson-AdzakuPenu/p/book/9781032975962)]​

## 10) Long‑form: _AI in the Creative Design Process: Guide (2026)_ – Parallel HQ (2025, guide)

## 4. Social media analytics & data‑driven optimization

Here the most actionable 2022–2026 material skews toward in‑depth guides rather than books, but they’re highly systemizable.

## 11) Long‑form: “YouTube Algorithm: How it Works & Tips to Optimize” – Sprout Social (2026)

- **Key takeaway:** Breaks down how the 2026 YouTube algorithm prioritizes watch time, CTR, engagement, and satisfaction, with optimization tactics.[[sproutsocial](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/youtube-algorithm/)]​
    
- **Relevance:** Direct inputs to your experimentation system: what metrics to track, what levers matter (titles, thumbnails, formats), and how Shorts impact discovery.[[sproutsocial](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/youtube-algorithm/)]​
    
- **eBook?:** Web guide; exportable to PDF.
    

## 12) Long‑form: “YouTube Algorithm 2026: How Does It Work and Tips to Optimize” – SocialPilot (2026)

- **Key takeaway:** Detailed explanation of recommendation factors (watch history, time/device, behavior signals, satisfaction surveys, evergreen relevance) and tactical optimization.[[socialpilot](https://www.socialpilot.co/youtube-marketing/youtube-algorithm)]​
    
- **Relevance:** Very useful for data‑driven optimization: it explains how user‑level behavior feeds into ranking, which informs what to A/B test and how to read analytics.[[socialpilot](https://www.socialpilot.co/youtube-marketing/youtube-algorithm)]​
    
- **eBook?:** Web article; saveable to PDF.
    

## 13) Long‑form: “How the YouTube Algorithm Works in 2026” – Dash Social (2025)

- **Key takeaway:** Historical evolution plus current ranking and recommendation logic, focused on using it to increase views, reach, and engagement.[[dashsocial](https://www.dashsocial.com/blog/how-the-youtube-algorithm-works)]​
    
- **Relevance:** Good complement for designing a testing roadmap around watch time, CTR, multi‑format strategy, and session retention.[[dashsocial](https://www.dashsocial.com/blog/how-the-youtube-algorithm-works)]​
    
- **eBook?:** Web article; export as PDF.
    

## 14) Long‑form: “The Ultimate Guide to AI‑Powered Social Media Marketing in 2025” – StarWeb Creative (2025)

- **Key takeaway:** Shows how to use AI for content variations, A/B testing, and analytics using tools like Sprout Social, Hootsuite Insights, and Brandwatch.[[starwebcreative](https://www.starwebcreative.com/blog/the-ultimate-guide-to-ai-powered-social-media-marketing-in-2025)]​

- **eBook?:** Web guide; can be saved to PDF.

- **Key takeaway:** Describes how AI bridges creative and analytic workflows, enabling continuous A/B testing of headlines/images and iterative optimization.[[roboticmarketer](https://www.roboticmarketer.com/data%E2%80%91driven-creativity-how-ai-will-transform-marketing-creativity-in-2026/)]​
